FAQ

Where can I register for the ACT/SAT?

Navigate to the useful links page to register for either exam.

Where will I be taking the exams?

After you start the registration process, it will ask you which facility you would like to take your exam.

How long are the segments for each exam?

The ACT has English, Mathematics, Reading, Science, and an optional Writing section

The SAT has Writing, Language, Mathematics, and a Reading section.

What is the time limit for each section and the amount of questions on the ACT?

English: 75 questions, in 45 minutes

Math: 60 questions, in 60 minutes

Reading: 40 questions, in 35 minutes

Science: 40 questions, in 35 minutes

Writing (optional): 1 essay, in 40 minutes

What is the time limit for each section and the amount of questions on the SAT?

Writing and Language: 44 questions, in 35 minutes

Math: 58 questions, in 80 minutes

Reading: 52 questions, in 65 minutes

Can I take both the SAT and the ACT?

Absolutely! We recommend taking both test because most of the time, students excel in one or the other. Mainly because some students have stronger time management skills which is what the ACT requires. While other students prefer having more time for each question but having harder questions with the SAT.

What year should I take the first exam?

You should shoot to take either the SAT or the ACT late in your sophomore year, especially if you are taking adcanced classes. This gives you the best opportunity to prepare yourself for success in later attempts of the exam. This will also give you a good feel as of what areas you are strong in and what areas you are weaker in as of now.
